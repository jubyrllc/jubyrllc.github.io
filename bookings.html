Equipment, catering, setup, promotion, and tax questions

View all booking FAQs

Money

When and how am I paid?

Door revenue is sent via Zelle within 24–48 hours of your event. Venue-sales payouts (your 15%/10% share) are processed by check the Thursday after your event and ready for pickup at the bar any time we’re open.

Do I need to submit a W-9?

Before your first payout — one W-9 on file covers every event we book you for going forward.

What happens if a payment is disputed or refunded?

Refunds, disputes, and chargebacks are deducted from your admission revenue before payout.

Ticketing

How many advance tickets may I sell?

Presale (advance) tickets are capped at 200 per event. That cap applies only to presale — it doesn’t limit total attendance.

Can I continue selling at the door?

Yes. Once presale is exhausted or you’ve hit 200, additional admission may be sold at the door, subject to the venue’s legal capacity and real-time availability.

What happens when the venue reaches capacity?

Admission is handled one-in-one-out. Once we reach legal capacity, admission is never guaranteed — even for guests who purchased in advance.

Planning

What equipment does Margolis provide?

A Pioneer XDJ-RX3, a Yamaha 12-channel mixer, one sub, four corner speakers, two projectors, and house XLRs. Door and security staff, bartenders, and basic furniture are included. No sound engineer is provided, microphones can’t be guaranteed, and all other cables are on you.

Can I use outside catering?

Yes, with advance approval. It may require additional fees, licensing, insurance, or other documentation, and outside alcohol is never permitted. Outside catering doesn’t grant access to Margolis’s kitchen.

What promotional support does Margolis provide?

One collaborative post, one weekly lineup post, and one day-of story post from @margolisphilly, plus a listing on our public events calendar. Flyer design, ticket sales, and the rest of your promotion are on you — we look for organizers prepared to go beyond posting a flyer.

When can I access the space?

Setup timing is arranged individually per event — we’ll confirm your access window once your booking is accepted.

What happens after I apply?

Jubyr LLC reviews your submission and verifies availability and applicable terms. You’ll receive written acceptance or follow-up questions — your date isn’t confirmed until written acceptance is issued. Once accepted, your booking and the policies you acknowledged become binding.

Private events · How the bar works

Five ways to run the bar.

Every private event picks a bar model — not sure, we’ll help you choose. Age requirements for private events depend on your event format and alcohol service plan — you’ll set that on the form below.

Most popular

Bar minimum

A spending minimum is set and guest purchases count toward it. Spend less, the host covers the difference; spend more, nothing extra beyond what’s purchased.

All-inclusive

Open bar

The host covers all beverages during the agreed service period. Full bar, or a curated selection of beer, wine, and cocktails.

Capped

Consumption tab

A card goes on file with a spending limit. Drinks charge to the tab until the limit is reached, then guests buy their own.

Curated

Limited consumption tab

Same as a consumption tab, but only selected beverages are covered — say, beer and wine or a signature cocktail menu.

Simple

Cash bar

Guests buy their own drinks all night. Nothing is charged to the host beyond applicable venue minimums or event requirements.

Read the full guidelines

Event timing. Friday and Saturday late-night events run 10:00 PM–2:00 AM. Fabrika hosts an earlier evening performance on these nights; between 9:00 and 10:00 PM the team performs a full room reset — dinner seating out, VIP tables set, bar and service stations prepped, stage and lighting finalized. Plan your arrival and setup accordingly.

Food. Late-night events can offer VIP table menu options and concession-style service. Food offerings must be confirmed before contract issuance so the kitchen can schedule staffing and prep.

Equipment. Fabrika provides a built-in professional sound system. Promoters and performers bring their own DJ equipment, controllers or mixers, laptops, adapters, and specialty gear — plus two XLR cables to connect to the house system. The technical team will assist with integration.

Promotion. No event promotion may begin until the contract is fully executed — no social posts, ticket sales, listings, or collaborations with Fabrika’s accounts before then.

Confirmation. Food and beverage services are finalized before contract issuance. Final details, timing, and logistics are outlined in the official agreement Fabrika issues after this intake form is submitted.

The presale, in full

Why we sell entry before your promo is ready.

Guests who love Margolis commit early — often before a night’s lineup, flyer, or ticket link exists. Without a presale, those guests drift to other plans and the night loses them for good. The $20 presale simply holds them for your event while your promo comes together, so neither you nor the venue loses potential guests while we wait.

It never touches your money. You keep 100% of your ticket sales and door sales, always. The presale ends the moment your lineup, flyer, and ticket link are approved — from then on every guest is sent to your link. Presale guests are venue-loyal turnout added on top of your crowd, not taken out of it.

Need a night without presale? For parties catered to strict demographics — LGBTQ-only nights, guaranteed sell-outs (with proof), private events, and similar — DJs and hosts may request that no presale run for their night. Margolis reserves the right to deny the request within reason. Ask by replying to your confirmation email or writing ruby@margolisphilly.com.
