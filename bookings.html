Public events · Our philosophy

You get paid. That’s the point.

You are the heartbeat of the room. Every host is paid through one of the most community-minded partnership models in the city.

Free events

20% of bar sales

Proportionate compensation for bringing business into the venue and helping create a successful night.

Ticketed events

100% of door + 10% of bar

Every dollar collected at the door is yours, plus a cut of the bar you helped fill. Ticket sales cap at 200 per event — everyone past that pays at the door, subject to capacity.

Talent perk

50% off food & drinks

DJs and performers get half off personal food and beverage purchases during their event.

Bar checksProcessed the Thursday of the week after your event
Door salesSent via Zelle within 24–48 hours

House equipment

What’s in the booth

Pioneer XDJ-RX3 · Yamaha 12-channel mixer · 1 sub · 4 corner speakers · 2 projectors · house XLRs. No sound engineer provided, microphones can’t be guaranteed, and all other cables are on you.

Advance guest reservations

We sell entry until your link is live.

Margolis reserves the right to sell advance guest reservations to your night up until the moment you provide your ticket link. Guests coming to our space shouldn’t have to wait on anyone to lock in their night, and we don’t want to miss a single opportunity for guests to enjoy our establishment. The moment your link is on file, every guest is sent to it instead — and you keep 100% of your ticket sales and all door sales, always.

Couches

Couch sales stay with the house.

Couch reservations are Margolis revenue for every event. Guests who purchase a couch with us have their cover waived and are added to the Margolis guest list for the night.

Couch guests & the bar

You still earn on their spend.

The $200 couch fee is Margolis revenue, no exceptions — but couch guests are still part of your night. Every dollar they spend on bottles and drinks at the bar counts toward your standard bar-sales percentage, same as any other guest.

Quick answers

The nuances hosts ask about most, without digging through every section above.

When do I actually get paid?

Bar checks are processed the Thursday of the week after your event and ready for pickup at the bar — check our events calendar and swing by any time we’re open. Door sales go out via Zelle within 24–48 hours of your event.

Do I keep 100% of my ticket and door sales?

Yes, always — even if Margolis has already sold advance guest reservations to your night before your ticket link went live. The moment your link is on file, we send every guest there instead.

Why would Margolis sell entry to my event before I do?

So no guest who wants to come to Margolis has to wait on a host to release tickets. It doesn’t cost you anything — you still keep 100% of your own ticket and door sales.

Do I get a cut of couch sales?

Not of the $200 reservation fee itself — that’s Margolis revenue, no exceptions. But couch guests are still your guests: their bottle service and bar tab count toward your standard bar-sales percentage for the night.

What happens if my night doesn’t hit the bar minimum?

You cover the shortage and forfeit payout for that event. Minimums are calculated on gross bar sales during official event hours — see the full breakdown below.

Do I need to submit a W9 every time I’m booked?

No. One W9 on file covers every event we book you for going forward.

Is there a fee on card payments at the door?

A 10% service charge applies to card transactions Jubyr LLC processes at the door. It’s waived only if you provide your own dedicated door staff and use a legitimate tap-to-pay platform like Square or Stripe, or mandatory pre-sale ticketing — Cash App and Venmo don’t qualify.

Private events · How the bar works

Five ways to run the bar.

Every private event picks a bar model — not sure, we’ll help you choose. Private events are the one exception to our 21+ rule: your guest list, your call.

Most popular

Bar minimum

A spending minimum is set and guest purchases count toward it. Spend less, the host covers the difference; spend more, nothing extra beyond what’s purchased.

All-inclusive

Open bar

The host covers all beverages during the agreed service period. Full bar, or a curated selection of beer, wine, and cocktails.

Capped

Consumption tab

A card goes on file with a spending limit. Drinks charge to the tab until the limit is reached, then guests buy their own.

Curated

Limited consumption tab

Same as a consumption tab, but only selected beverages are covered — say, beer and wine or a signature cocktail menu.

Simple

Cash bar

Guests buy their own drinks all night. Nothing is charged to the host beyond applicable venue minimums or event requirements.

Undecided

Not sure yet

Pick this on the form and we’ll figure out the best model together.

Read the full guidelines

Event timing. Friday and Saturday late-night events run 10:00 PM–2:00 AM. Fabrika hosts an earlier evening performance on these nights; between 9:00 and 10:00 PM the team performs a full room reset — dinner seating out, VIP tables set, bar and service stations prepped, stage and lighting finalized. Plan your arrival and setup accordingly.

Food. Late-night events can offer VIP table menu options and concession-style service. Food offerings must be confirmed before contract issuance so the kitchen can schedule staffing and prep.

Equipment. Fabrika provides a built-in professional sound system. Promoters and performers bring their own DJ equipment, controllers or mixers, laptops, adapters, and specialty gear — plus two XLR cables to connect to the house system. The technical team will assist with integration.

Promotion. No event promotion may begin until the contract is fully executed — no social posts, ticket sales, listings, or collaborations with Fabrika’s accounts before then.

Confirmation. Food and beverage services are finalized before contract issuance. Final details, timing, and logistics are outlined in the official agreement Fabrika issues after this intake form is submitted.
